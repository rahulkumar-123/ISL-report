2. Module 1: Advanced Drone Technology

A foundational exploration of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), from physical assembly and electronic integration to the complexities of autonomous programming.

Hands-on Mechanical Assembly

The module began with an immersive hands-on workshop to build a quadcopter from a kit. This provided a practical, tactile understanding of the core components and their intricate interplay, moving beyond textbook diagrams.

  • Frame & Motors: Assembled a carbon fiber frame, understanding the trade-offs between weight, strength, and vibration damping. Mounted and secured brushless DC motors, ensuring proper alignment.
  • Electronics Integration: Carefully wired the Electronic Speed Controllers (ESCs), Power Distribution Board, and the Flight Controller, learning the importance of clean soldering, heat shrinking, and secure connections to prevent in-flight failures.
  • Calibration & Setup: Performed crucial calibration of the ESCs and configured the flight controller software (e.g., Betaflight), a vital step to ensure the drone interprets commands correctly for stable flight.
  • Propulsion Dynamics: Learned about LiPo battery safety protocols, C-ratings (discharge capacity), and the physics of propeller design based on Blade Element Momentum Theory, understanding how pitch and diameter affect thrust and efficiency.

Autonomous Navigation Concepts

We then transitioned from hardware to software, exploring the complex algorithms and programming frameworks that enable intelligent, autonomous flight without direct human control.

  • Sensor Fusion: Delved into how the flight controller fuses data from the Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU - accelerometer and gyroscope), GPS, and barometers using algorithms like Kalman filters to achieve an accurate and reliable estimation of its position and orientation, even with noisy sensor data.
  • PID Control Theory: Learned the theory behind tuning Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controllers, understanding how each gain (P, I, D) affects the drone's response to disturbances for crisp, stable flight without oscillation.
  • Path Planning Algorithms: Studied key algorithms like Dijkstra's, A*, and RRT for navigating complex environments, understanding their computational costs, benefits, and suitability for different scenarios (e.g., known maps vs. unknown terrain).
  • Programming Implementation: Explored how these concepts are implemented using high-level languages like Python with frameworks such as the Robot Operating System (ROS) for modular and scalable drone applications.

3. Module 2: CanSat & CubeSat Development

A simulated end-to-end satellite mission, providing deep insight into professional engineering lifecycles, systems engineering, and collaborative project management.

Team Project: "Project VAYU"

Our team was tasked with designing, building, and testing a CanSat to measure atmospheric data during descent. This project was a masterclass in systems thinking, collaborative problem-solving, and the iterative design process essential for any engineering project.

Mission Details

  • Objective: Measure temperature, pressure, and humidity during descent to gather a high-resolution atmospheric profile for local weather modeling, and transmit it to a ground station.
  • My Role: Responsible for the Payload (BME280 sensor) and Communications (LoRa radio) subsystems. This included writing the driver code for the sensor, integrating it with the microcontroller, and developing the data packetization protocol for transmission.
  • Key Challenge: Managing the high instantaneous current draw from the radio's transmission bursts, which caused voltage drops across the system and led to intermittent resets of our microcontroller.
  • Solution: We implemented a two-fold solution: adding decoupling capacitors to the power circuit to provide a local reservoir of charge, and optimizing the software to enter a deep-sleep state between readings to conserve overall energy.

4. Module 3: Rocketry and Launch Vehicle Dynamics

An introduction to the fundamental challenge of space exploration: developing the immense thrust required to overcome Earth's gravity and achieve orbit.

Fundamentals of Rocket Science

  • Tsiolkovsky Rocket Equation: Studied the core relationship between a rocket's mass ratio, its engine's exhaust velocity (Specific Impulse or Isp), and its total change in velocity (delta-v). This gave a clear intuition for why multistage rockets are necessary for orbital missions.
  • Propulsion Systems: Compared the characteristics of solid propellant engines (high thrust, simple, reliable) and liquid propellant engines (higher efficiency, throttling, restart capability), understanding their use cases in different launch stages.
  • Aerodynamic Stability: Learned that the Center of Pressure (the net point of aerodynamic forces) must be located behind the Center of Gravity to ensure the rocket flies straight and does not tumble, a key principle in rocket design.

Model Rocketry Workshop

The highlight was building and launching a model rocket using standard Estes engines. Before launch, we used simulation software like OpenRocket to predict the flight path, apogee, and verify stability. The successful launch and parachute recovery was a powerful lesson in precision, safety protocols, and the physics of flight, turning abstract equations into a tangible, thrilling event.

5. Module 4: Remote Sensing & GIS Applications

Demonstrating how data from space can be processed, analyzed, and visualized to solve critical real-world problems on Earth.

Case Study: Urban Heat Island Analysis

We used open-source GIS software (QGIS) and Landsat 8 satellite data from the USGS EarthExplorer portal to analyze the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect in a major Indian city. This involved processing raw thermal and multispectral imagery to derive actionable environmental insights.

Methodology & Findings

  • LST Calculation: Processed the satellite's thermal band data, applying atmospheric corrections to create an accurate Land Surface Temperature map, showing hot and cool spots across the city.
  • NDVI Calculation: Used the red and near-infrared bands to calculate the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index, creating a map showing the density and health of green cover.
  • Outcome: By spatially comparing the two maps, we established a clear and strong inverse correlation: areas with less vegetation (dense urban cores, industrial areas) had significantly higher surface temperatures. This demonstrates how remote sensing can provide critical data for urban planners to design cooler, more sustainable cities.

6. Module 5: The New Space Economy & Entrepreneurship

Contextualizing our technical training within the broader landscape of the global and Indian space industry, focusing on emerging commercial opportunities.

India's Growing Space Ecosystem

We studied the paradigm shift in India's space sector, driven by policy reforms and the establishment of IN-SPACe as a single-window agency to empower private entities. We discussed the symbiotic relationship between the legacy expertise of ISRO and the agility of a new wave of private startups like Skyroot Aerospace and Pixxel.

Career Pathways in New Space

Explored diverse career roles beyond traditional engineering, including Data Science for analyzing satellite imagery, Robotics and AI for autonomous rovers, Systems Engineering for managing mission complexity, and even specialized fields like Business Development and international Space Law.
